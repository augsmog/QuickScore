### 1.1 — 18Birdies

| Metric | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Founded | 2014 |
| Users | Millions (exact undisclosed) |
| Revenue | $15.9M annually (2025) |
| Funding | Angel round (2017) + Decathlon Capital Partners (2022) |

- Built 6 unique leaderboards (scoring, handicap, stats, par, birdie count) enabling friend and global competition

- A/B test feature visibility during onboarding to find high-impact, low-adoption features

| Metric | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Founded | 2010 |
| Users | 65M+ downloads |
| Organic share | 95% organic growth |
| Funding | $155M Series D (2021) |

**What's replicable for ScoreSnap:**
- Accuracy is everything — invest in OCR/scanning quality until results are consistently good
- Build the review/engagement flywheel: scan → stats → share → compare → return
- Social moment: scanning a scorecard at the 19th hole is inherently social
- Crowdsource course data to build a moat

### 2.6 — Scan App Synthesis: The Viral Loop Blueprint

**Universal pattern across all successful scan apps:**

```
SCAN → ACCURATE RESULT → SOCIAL MOMENT → SHARE → NEW USER → SCAN
```

**Key variables that determine growth velocity:**

1. **Accuracy threshold** — Vivino accelerated at ~70% accuracy. PhotoMath needed near-100% for math. ScoreSnap needs >95% scorecard recognition
2. **Social moment quality** — Higher when scanning happens in groups (golf foursome > solo wine drinker > solo student)
3. **Share incentive** — Competitive data (scores, handicap) > informational data (song name) > utilitarian data (document PDF)
4. **Time to value** — PhotoMath: <1 second. Shazam: 3–5 seconds. Target: <5 seconds for ScoreSnap

### 5.4 — UGC Strategy for ScoreSnap

### 6.1 — 2025 ASO Critical Elements

- Apps under 3.5 stars barely rank

- Apple's in-app events

- Custom Store Listings (Google Play) and Custom Product Pages (iOS) for audience-specific targeting

## 8. Golf Season Timing & Optimal Launch Window

### 8.1 — Seasonal Participation Data

| Month | Activity Level | Notes |
|-------|---------------|-------|
| January | Low | Off-season (except Southern US). New Year = resolution energy |
| February | Low–Medium | Early birds in warm states. Pre-season planning begins |
| **March** | **Medium–High** | **Season starts. Course promotions launch. Equipment shopping peaks** |
| **April** | **High** | **Masters buzz. Full season swing in most regions** |
| **May–August** | **Peak** | **Maximum rounds played. Peak engagement** |
| September | High | Fall golf. Pleasant weather in most regions |
| October | Medium–High | Season winding down in North. Still active in South |
| November | Medium | Black Friday golf deals. Southern play continues |
| December | Low | Lowest volume month. Holiday gift season |

**Regional variation:**
- 18 US states offer year-round golf (FL, CA, AZ, TX, etc.)
- Northern states (MI, MN, WI, IL): May–October season
- Warm states grew faster in 2024: Nevada +8%, Georgia +13%, Texas +13%, Hawaii +27%
- December 2024: NY, NJ, PA saw 58% YoY drop due to winter weather

---

### 8.2 — Optimal Launch Window for ScoreSnap

**Recommended: Late February / Early March launch**

**Why this window is optimal:**

1. **Season activation** — Golfers are excited, planning their first rounds, downloading apps, shopping for equipment
2. **Quieter app store** — Late January through mid-February has less competition; apps launched in Q1 show 35% higher 6-month retention
3. **Masters anticipation** — April Masters buzz amplifies all golf content
4. **Full season runway** — Launch gives 6–8 months of peak season to build word-of-mouth before winter decline
5. **Course partnerships** — Golf courses launching spring promotions are receptive to new partnerships
6. **Media coverage** — Golf media ramps up coverage for season start

**Pre-launch timeline:**
- **December–January:** Beta testing with 50–100 golfers. Finalize ASO. Seed to influencers
- **February (first 2 weeks):** Soft launch. Collect reviews. Iterate on feedback
- **Late February / Early March:** Full launch with PR push, influencer content, ASO optimization
- **April:** Masters-timed content marketing push
- **May–August:** Sustained growth through peak season

**Avoid:**
- July–August launch (summer vacation = lower app discovery)
- November–December launch (season ending in most regions; holiday noise)

---

### 8.3 — Weekly Timing

- Most app downloads occur Tuesday–Thursday
- Weekends drop by ~25%
- Plan App Store updates for Wednesdays to maximize editorial/algorithmic visibility

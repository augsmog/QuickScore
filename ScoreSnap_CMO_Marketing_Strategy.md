## 1. CMO Vision & Operating Model

### Go-to-Market Thesis

ScoreSnap's core mechanic is inherently viral in a way that no other golf app can replicate. Every time a golfer scans a scorecard at the end of a round, three other people in the foursome watch it happen. That scan is a live product demo — the camera recognizes the card, scores populate instantly, game modes calculate who won and who owes what, and a settlement flow tells everyone to pay up. The three people watching didn't plan to evaluate a new app that day. They just saw magic happen and want it.

This is the thesis: ScoreSnap doesn't need to find its users. Its users find each other. The product is the marketing. Our job as the marketing organization is to accelerate what the product already does naturally — create moments of delight that spread from person to person, foursome to foursome, course to course.

Three structural advantages make this possible. First, the scan-and-settle loop creates network dependency. When a settlement notification arrives — "Mike owes you $5 from Saturday's Nassau" — the recipient must install the app to see and act on it. This is the same mechanic that powered Venmo and Cash App's early growth: install-to-receive-money. Second, the 25+ game mode library creates depth that drives retention. Most golfers know Skins and Nassau. When ScoreSnap introduces them to Wolf, Bingo Bango Bongo, or Dots, it creates discovery moments that are inherently shareable ("have you tried this game?"). Third, golf is seasonal and social. The sport is played in groups, at courses, during a defined season. This creates natural clustering: a golfer who loves ScoreSnap plays 2–3 times per week with overlapping groups, seeding the app across multiple social circles within weeks.

### Operating Philosophy

This marketing organization runs like a product team. We ship experiments, measure results, and iterate weekly. We don't do "campaigns" in the traditional sense — we run hypotheses. Every piece of content, every feature announcement, every influencer activation has a measurable outcome attached to it. We move fast, we learn fast, we kill what doesn't work without sentiment.

Core principles:

- **Product-led growth above all.** If we have to explain why the app is good, the app isn't good enough. Marketing amplifies product excellence; it doesn't compensate for product gaps.
- **Compound over linear.** We prioritize initiatives that build on themselves — viral loops, community, SEO — over one-shot tactics.
- **Measure everything, but obsess over the right things.** Vanity metrics (impressions, followers) are inputs. Output metrics (installs, D7 retention, K-factor) are what we optimize for.

### Key Metrics & OKRs

**Primary OKR:** 100,000 cumulative downloads by September 30, 2026.

Supporting metrics:

- **DAU/MAU ratio** — target 25%+ (indicates habitual use during golf season)
- **D7 retention** — target 40%+ (user scanned at least twice in first week)
- **D30 retention** — target 20%+ (user is an active golfer using ScoreSnap regularly)
- **Premium conversion rate** — target 5% of active users within 60 days
- **Viral coefficient (K-factor)** — target 0.7+ (each user brings 0.7 new users on average)
- **Time to first scan** — target under 90 seconds from download
- **Settlement completion rate** — % of game results where all players have installed and settled

The only "spend" is opportunity cost of the team's time and potentially small influencer gifting (merch, premium codes).

## 2. Team Structure — Six Department Leads

### VP of Growth & Virality

**Scope and Ownership:** Owns the viral engine — referral mechanics, share flows, network effects, invite systems, settlement-driven acquisition. Responsible for the K-factor metric above all else. Works closely with product/engineering to ensure viral loops are embedded in the core experience, not bolted on.

**Strategy with Tactical Plays:**

1. **Settlement-as-Acquisition Loop.** The single most powerful growth mechanic. When a round ends and ScoreSnap calculates that Player B owes Player A $8 from a Nassau, Player A sends a settlement request. If Player B doesn't have the app, they receive an SMS/iMessage with a deeplink: "You owe Alex $8 from Saturday's round at Pinehurst. Tap to see the breakdown and settle up." This is not a marketing message — it's a utility notification with real social stakes. The install motivation isn't "try this cool app," it's "see what you owe your friend." Model: Venmo's "install to receive money" mechanic, which drove their college campus expansion. Cash App's data showed retention jumps 31 percentage points when a user has 4+ friends on the platform. ScoreSnap's target: get all four members of a regular foursome installed within 2 rounds.

2. **Shareable Results Cards.** After every round, ScoreSnap generates a visual results card — a designed, branded graphic showing game outcomes (who won Skins, the Nassau breakdown, net scores). This card is optimized for iMessage and Instagram Stories. It includes a subtle "Scored with ScoreSnap" watermark and a QR code / App Store link. Model: Wordle's emoji grid, which drove the game from 90 players to 2 million in 3 months through organic sharing. The grid worked because it was spoiler-free, visually interesting, and created FOMO. ScoreSnap's results card serves the same function: it's interesting to golfers who see it, it implies a product they don't have, and it invites curiosity.

3. **Group Invite Flow.** Before a round, a user can create a game (e.g., "Saturday 8am Skins at Pebble Beach") and invite their foursome via text. Non-users receive a branded invite link with the game details and a one-tap install flow. After installing, they're automatically added to the game group. This reduces friction from "download an app and figure it out" to "tap this link and you're in." Model: Houseparty's room invite mechanic, where receiving an invite was the primary acquisition channel.

4. **"Invite 3, Get Premium" Referral Program.** Simple mechanic: invite 3 friends who each complete at least one scan, and you get 1 month of premium free. This aligns incentives (the inviter wants their playing partners on the app anyway) and puts a tangible reward on organic behavior. Model: Dropbox's referral program (invite friends, get free storage) which drove 3,900% growth over 15 months.

5. **Settlement Push Notifications as Re-engagement.** For users who haven't opened the app in 2+ weeks: "Your last round at [Course Name] — you never settled up with your group. Tap to review." This uses real social context (an actual round they played) as a re-engagement hook that feels useful, not spammy.

**Weekly/Monthly Deliverables:** Weekly K-factor report, referral funnel metrics (invites sent → installs → first scans), settlement completion rate dashboard. Monthly: viral loop A/B test results, cohort analysis of referred users vs. organic.

**Case Studies to Model:**

- **Cash App:** Their $5-for-$5 referral program scaled to $1B+ in referral spend because LTV justified it. Key learning: the referral reward should feel generous relative to the ask. For ScoreSnap, 1 month of premium ($4.99 value) for 3 referrals is the right ratio.
- **Wordle:** The emoji share grid was never planned as a growth feature — a user suggested it on Twitter and the developer added it in a day. Lesson: build the simplest possible share mechanic first, then iterate based on what users actually share.
- **NGL:** Hit 250 million downloads with zero marketing spend by designing the product so that every use automatically created content on Instagram Stories that drove more installs. Their North Star metric was "reply rate" — the equivalent for ScoreSnap is "settlement completion rate."

**Self-Improving Mechanism:** Track K-factor weekly. For every share mechanic (results card, invite link, settlement notification), measure: impressions → taps → installs → first scans. A/B test card designs, invite copy, and notification timing. Kill any mechanic with a tap-to-install rate below 5%.

**Experiment Backlog:**

- H1: Results cards with specific dollar amounts ("You won $12!") drive more shares than generic cards ("You won Skins!")
- H2: SMS settlement notifications convert at 2x the rate of email notifications
- H3: "Invite 3, Get Premium" drives more installs than "Invite 1, Get 1 Week Free"
- H4: Pre-round game creation invites convert better than post-round settlement invites
- H5: Adding the losing player's reaction emoji to results cards increases share rate

### VP of Content & Community

**Scope and Ownership:** Owns all organic social media (TikTok, Instagram, YouTube Shorts), user-generated content strategy, community platforms (Discord, Reddit), and the ScoreSnap brand voice on social. Responsible for building an audience that creates content for us, not just consumes ours.

**Strategy with Tactical Plays:**

1. **"ScoreSnap Moments" Content Format.** The signature content piece: film the moment a group scans their scorecard and discovers who won (and who owes). Capture genuine reactions — the trash talk, the disbelief, the celebration. This is inherently entertaining because golf betting creates real emotional stakes. Format: vertical video, 15–30 seconds, trending audio, text overlay showing the game mode and dollar amounts. Post across TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts simultaneously.

2. **"Game Mode of the Week" Series.** Most golfers only know Skins and Nassau. ScoreSnap supports 25+ game modes. Each week, create a short explainer video introducing a game mode (Wolf, Bingo Bango Bongo, Hammer, Dots, etc.) with on-course footage showing how it works and why it's fun. This educates users, drives feature discovery, and gives existing users a reason to try something new on their next round.

3. **UGC Amplification Engine.** Create a branded hashtag (#ScoreSnapMoment) and actively repost the best user-submitted content. Run monthly "Best ScoreSnap Moment" contests where the winner gets a year of premium free. The goal is to make users feel like creators, not consumers. Provide a simple "share your moment" flow in the app that exports a vertical video template with ScoreSnap branding.

4. **Discord Community for Golf Degenerates.** Build a Discord server for ScoreSnap users focused on golf betting culture. Channels: game mode strategies, scorecard flex (post your best rounds), feature requests, local group matchmaking. This creates a direct feedback loop with the most engaged users and gives them ownership over the product's direction.

5. **Reddit r/golf Presence.** r/golf has 1.1M+ members and is one of the most active sports subreddits. Become a genuine participant (not a spammer) by posting useful content: game mode explainers, interesting scoring stats, and responding to threads about golf betting. When users organically mention ScoreSnap, the community presence validates it.

**Weekly/Monthly Deliverables:** 5 TikToks/week, 3 Instagram Reels/week, 1 YouTube Short/week, daily Discord engagement, weekly Reddit participation. Monthly: content performance report (views, engagement rate, profile visits, app store click-throughs), UGC volume tracker.

**Case Studies to Model:**

- **Duolingo TikTok:** Grew from zero to 16+ million TikTok followers by giving their mascot (Duo the Owl) an unhinged, meme-native personality. Their engagement rate (~11%) is 4–5x the platform average. Key learning: brand personality beats production value. ScoreSnap's social voice should be the trash-talking golf buddy everyone loves to play with — confident, funny, slightly obnoxious, always keeping score. Duolingo's "Death of Duo" campaign drove a 25,000% increase in brand mentions and boosted downloads with zero ad spend.
- **Good Good Golf:** 2M YouTube subscribers built on the format of friends competing and gambling on the course — literally ScoreSnap's use case filmed. Their success proves the audience exists and is massive. Key learning: authenticity and group dynamics are more compelling than polished production.
- **Strava's Community Features:** 130M+ users, 14 billion "Kudos" interactions in 2025 alone. Strava proved that social features in a fitness/sport app drive retention better than any notification strategy. Their data shows athletes exercise longer and more frequently when connected to friends on the platform. Key learning: community isn't a marketing channel — it's a retention engine.

**Self-Improving Mechanism:** Track views and engagement rate per content type (ScoreSnap Moments vs. Game Mode explainers vs. UGC reposts). Double down on the top-performing format each month. Track the funnel from social impression → profile visit → link click → App Store → install. Identify which content types drive actual installs, not just views.

**Experiment Backlog:**

- H1: "Reaction reveal" videos (filming the moment someone discovers they lost $20) outperform instructional content 3:1 on engagement
- H2: Posting at 6–8pm (post-round hours) drives 50% more engagement than morning posts
- H3: User-submitted content outperforms brand-produced content on TikTok
- H4: Game mode explainers with on-course footage outperform animated/screen-recorded explainers
- H5: Discord members have 2x higher D30 retention than non-members

### VP of Influencer & Partnerships

**Scope and Ownership:** Owns golf influencer relationships (YouTube, TikTok, Instagram), course partnerships, golf league adoption, charity tournament integrations, and co-marketing opportunities. Responsible for generating installs through trusted voices and institutional channels.

**Strategy with Tactical Plays:**

1. **Micro-Influencer Seeding Program.** Identify 50 golf micro-influencers (10K–100K followers) across TikTok, YouTube, and Instagram. Send each a "ScoreSnap Challenge" kit: a premium account, a branded scorecard holder, and a simple brief ("film your group scanning your scorecard and reacting to the results"). No script, no talking points — just authentic use. Compensate with premium lifetime access and featured placement in the app. Micro-influencers in golf are highly trusted and significantly cheaper than mega-influencers. Target golf betting content creators specifically.

2. **"ScoreSnap Saturdays" Course Partnerships.** Partner with 10 local courses to promote ScoreSnap on specific Saturdays. The course announces it via email/signage, the pro shop staff demos the app at check-in, and players scan their cards at the end of the round. Incentive for courses: ScoreSnap promotes the course in the app's course directory and social channels. Start with public courses near major metros (affordable tee times = higher volume = more installs per event).

3. **Golf League Adoption Program.** Target recreational golf league organizers — the person who runs the weekly men's league, the couples' league, the company league. Offer ScoreSnap as the official scoring and settlement tool. Each league represents 20–100 guaranteed installs with high retention (they play weekly). Provide a simple "league admin" view (exportable leaderboards, season standings). Outreach through GolfWRX forums, local Facebook groups, and direct contact through course pro shops.

4. **Charity Tournament Integration.** Golf charity tournaments are a massive market (an estimated 12,000+ annually in the US). ScoreSnap can position as the free scoring solution for charity events. Each tournament = 72–144 players (18–36 foursomes), all installing the app on the same day. Create a "Tournament Mode" in the app with bulk scoring features. Market through tournament organizer networks and golf event platforms.

5. **Good Good / Creator Collaboration.** The long-shot, high-impact play: get Good Good or a similar top-tier golf creator to feature ScoreSnap in a video. Don't pitch it as a sponsorship — pitch it as a content format. "Play a round with 5 game modes running simultaneously, scan the card at the end, reveal who owes what." This is content they'd want to make because it creates drama and stakes. A single Good Good video could drive 50K+ installs.

**Weekly/Monthly Deliverables:** Weekly outreach tracker (influencers contacted, response rate, content posted). Monthly: installs attributed to influencer content (tracked via unique referral codes), partnership pipeline status, content assets generated from activations.

**Case Studies to Model:**

- **Whoop's Athlete Seeding:** Gave free bands to athletes and fitness influencers who became organic advocates. They didn't pay for posts — they gave a valuable product to people who would naturally talk about it. The authenticity of unpaid advocacy drove trust and conversion. ScoreSnap's equivalent: lifetime premium for influencers who genuinely love golf betting.
- **Calm's Sports Partnerships:** Partnered with athletes and sports teams to reach audiences through trusted voices. Their LeBron James sleep stories generated massive earned media. Key learning: align with voices your audience already trusts. For ScoreSnap, that's golf creators, not generic lifestyle influencers.

**Self-Improving Mechanism:** Track installs per influencer by tier (micro 10K–50K, mid 50K–200K, macro 200K+). Calculate effective CPA by tier. Track content lifespan (how long does an influencer post continue driving installs?). Double down on the tier and content format that delivers the lowest CPA.

**Experiment Backlog:**

- H1: Micro-influencers (10K–50K followers) deliver lower CPA than mid-tier (50K–200K)
- H2: "Reaction to scanning" content drives more installs than "app walkthrough" content
- H3: Course partnership Saturdays drive 50+ installs per event
- H4: League adoption converts at 80%+ of league members within 3 weeks
- H5: Charity tournament integration drives installs at < $0.50 effective CPA (measured by ScoreSnap's time investment)

### VP of Product Marketing & ASO

**Scope and Ownership:** Owns App Store presence (listing, screenshots, keywords, ratings/reviews), product positioning and messaging, the ScoreSnap landing page (scoresnap.app), competitive positioning, and launch messaging. Responsible for converting App Store impressions into installs at the highest possible rate.

**Strategy with Tactical Plays:**

1. **Keyword Domination Strategy.** ASO is the single highest-leverage free acquisition channel for a utility app. Target long-tail keywords with high intent and low competition: "golf scorecard app," "golf betting app," "golf skins calculator," "nassau golf scoring," "golf game modes," "golf scorecard scanner," "golf betting calculator," "wolf golf game." Use the app title and subtitle to pack in primary keywords: "ScoreSnap: Golf Scorecard Scanner" with subtitle "Scan, Score & Settle Golf Bets." Monitor keyword rankings weekly and iterate.

2. **Screenshot A/B Testing.** The first three screenshots drive the majority of App Store conversion. Test two approaches: (A) Lead with the scan flow — show a phone scanning a physical scorecard with results appearing instantly. (B) Lead with game mode results — show the settlement screen ("You won $12 from Skins!"). Hypothesis: the scan flow screenshot converts better because it communicates the unique value proposition immediately. Run tests monthly using App Store Connect's product page optimization.

3. **Landing Page (scoresnap.app).** A single-page site with: hero section (tagline + App Store badge + 15-second demo video), how it works (3-step: Scan → Score → Settle), game modes showcase, testimonials/reviews, email capture for updates. Optimize for SEO around the same keywords as ASO. The landing page serves as a credibility anchor for PR, influencer, and social traffic that doesn't go directly to the App Store.

4. **Ratings & Reviews Engine.** Prompt users for App Store reviews at the optimal moment: immediately after a successful scan where they won money. "You just won $8 from Skins! Enjoying ScoreSnap? Rate us on the App Store." This captures users at peak satisfaction. Target 4.7+ stars within the first 1,000 reviews. High ratings dramatically improve App Store conversion and search ranking.

5. **Competitive Positioning.** Position ScoreSnap in a new category: not a "golf GPS app" (competing with SwingU, 18Birdies, Hole19) but a "golf betting and scoring app" (a category ScoreSnap can own). All messaging should reinforce this positioning: "The app that settles your golf bets" rather than "another golf app."

**Weekly/Monthly Deliverables:** Weekly keyword ranking report (track top 20 target keywords), monthly screenshot A/B test results, weekly App Store conversion rate (impression → product page view → install), landing page analytics (traffic, bounce rate, App Store clicks). Monthly: competitive positioning audit (are competitors targeting our keywords?).

**Case Studies to Model:**

- **Flighty:** Dominated the flight tracking niche with design-forward App Store screenshots and precise keyword targeting. They didn't compete with "travel apps" — they owned "flight tracker." Key learning: niche positioning + beautiful screenshots + keyword precision = category ownership. ScoreSnap should own "golf betting app."
- **Widgetsmith:** 131 million downloads, driven initially by a TikTok viral moment that aligned with an iOS platform change. The developer, David Smith, had built 59 apps before Widgetsmith broke through. Key learning: preparation meets opportunity. ScoreSnap's opportunity is Masters Week + golf season. The app must be polished and the ASO must be optimized before the moment arrives.
- **Sensor Tower ASO case studies:** Show that optimized listings see 300–700% increases in organic downloads. The basics (keyword research, screenshot optimization, localization) compound over time.

**Self-Improving Mechanism:** Track conversion rate at every stage: Search impression → Product page view → Install. If impressions are high but conversions are low, the screenshots/description need work. If impressions are low, keywords need work. Run monthly A/B tests on screenshots, icon, and description. Track which keywords drive actual installs (not just impressions) using App Store Connect's source data.

**Experiment Backlog:**

- H1: "Scan flow" screenshots convert 20% better than "settlement results" screenshots
- H2: Adding "Free" to the subtitle increases conversion rate by 15%
- H3: The keyword "golf betting app" drives more qualified installs than "golf scorecard app"
- H4: A 15-second app preview video in the listing increases conversion by 25%
- H5: Prompting for reviews after a "you won" moment yields 3x more 5-star reviews than a generic prompt

### VP of Lifecycle & Retention

**Scope and Ownership:** Owns the user journey from download through long-term retention: onboarding flow, push notification strategy, email lifecycle, churn prevention, and premium conversion. Responsible for ensuring that acquired users become retained users who generate lifetime value.

**Strategy with Tactical Plays:**

1. **"90-Second Magic" Onboarding.** The first scan must happen within 90 seconds of download. The onboarding flow: open app → see a sample scorecard image → guided camera overlay shows how to position the card → scan completes → game modes calculate → results appear with dollar amounts. The user should feel the "wow, this actually works" moment before they even create an account. Delay account creation until after the first scan — let the value lead. Model: Shazam's onboarding was simply "open the app and hold it up to music." No tutorial, no signup, just instant value.

2. **Progressive Game Mode Discovery.** Most golfers know 2–3 game modes. ScoreSnap supports 25+. Don't overwhelm new users with all 25 on day one. After the first scan, show results for the 3 most common modes (Skins, Nassau, Match Play). After the second scan, suggest 2 new modes: "Based on your group's scores, you would have loved playing Wolf — here's how it works." This creates a discovery engine that keeps the app fresh across dozens of rounds. Each new game mode is a reason to play again and a topic to discuss with playing partners.

3. **Contextual Push Notifications.** Push notifications must feel useful, not promotional. "It's Saturday morning and 72° — perfect golf weather. Ready to ScoreSnap your round?" sent at 7am on weekend mornings during golf season. "You played Skins last 3 rounds. Try Wolf this weekend — it's chaos and your group will love it." "Your group from last Saturday hasn't settled up yet. Send a reminder?" These notifications use real context (weather, play history, unsettled games) to feel like a helpful caddie, not a marketing machine.

4. **Win-Back Sequences.** For users who haven't scanned in 14+ days: Day 14 push: "Miss the competition? Your group is waiting." Day 21 email: "3 new game modes added since your last round — here's what you're missing." Day 30 push: "We saved your last round's data. Come back and settle up." For users who scanned once and never returned: trigger a "first round follow-up" email within 24 hours asking what went wrong (survey), offering tips for a better experience, and highlighting game modes they didn't try.

5. **Premium Conversion Triggers.** The free tier includes 1 scan and 7 game modes. Premium unlocks unlimited scans and 25+ modes. Conversion triggers: after the free scan, show results for 3 premium game modes grayed out with "Unlock with Premium — you would have won $4 from Dots." This creates loss aversion (you won money you can't see). After 3 rounds with the same group, prompt: "Your regular foursome is on ScoreSnap. Upgrade to track season-long standings." Offer a 7-day free trial after the first scan to let users experience full functionality during their next round.

**Weekly/Monthly Deliverables:** Funnel metrics (download → first scan → second scan → premium), D1/D7/D30 retention curves, push notification open rates and action rates, email open/click rates, churn cohort analysis. Monthly: onboarding A/B test results, notification timing optimization report.

**Case Studies to Model:**

- **Headspace:** Perfected progressive onboarding — free users get immediate value (3-minute meditation) before being asked to commit. Their Day 1 retention is among the highest in the wellness category. Key learning: deliver value before asking for anything. ScoreSnap should scan before requiring an account.
- **Duolingo's Streak Mechanic:** The daily streak is Duolingo's most powerful retention tool. Losing a streak creates emotional cost that drives daily opens. ScoreSnap's equivalent: "Round Streaks" — track consecutive weeks with a ScoreSnap round. After 4 weeks: "You've ScoreSnapped 4 rounds in a row!" Missing a week: "Your streak is at risk!" This works because golfers who play weekly are the core audience.
- **Strava's Social Hooks:** 83% of surveyed Strava users reported being more motivated to exercise because of the app's community features. Social visibility (friends seeing your activity) drives engagement more effectively than any notification. ScoreSnap should show group activity — "Alex just scanned a round at Oak Hill — 78 gross. Think you can beat it?"

**Self-Improving Mechanism:** A/B test every onboarding screen, notification, and email. Track the impact of each notification type on 7-day re-engagement rate. Segment users by frequency (weekly golfers vs. occasional) and optimize lifecycle flows for each segment. Monthly: review churn surveys to identify the top 3 reasons users leave and build mitigation features.

**Experiment Backlog:**

- H1: Showing a demo scan during onboarding (before the user's own scan) increases first-scan completion by 30%
- H2: Delaying account creation until after first scan increases D1 retention by 20%
- H3: "You would have won $X" premium upsell converts 2x better than "Unlock more game modes"
- H4: Saturday 7am push notifications have 3x the open rate of weekday notifications
- H5: "Round Streak" mechanic increases weekly active rate by 15%

### VP of PR & Earned Media

**Scope and Ownership:** Owns press coverage, podcast appearances, Product Hunt and Hacker News launches, golf media relationships, and all earned media initiatives. Responsible for generating credibility, awareness, and traffic spikes through editorial coverage and word-of-mouth among media and tastemakers.

**Strategy with Tactical Plays:**

1. **Pre-Launch Waitlist with "Founding Member" Positioning.** Before the App Store launch, build a landing page waitlist. Positioning: "Be one of the first 1,000 ScoreSnap Founding Members." Founding Members get: lifetime premium access, name on the "Founders Wall" in the app, early access to new game modes, and a direct Slack/Discord channel with the development team. This creates exclusivity, builds an email list for launch day, and seeds early advocates who feel ownership. Model: Arc browser's waitlist created scarcity and word-of-mouth buzz that generated hundreds of thousands of signups before launch.

2. **Masters Week Launch Blitz.** Time the public launch to Masters Week (April 10–13, 2026). Coordinate a simultaneous press embargo lift, influencer content drop, Product Hunt launch, and social media activation. Pitch angle for sports/golf media: "The app that finally settles your golf bets — just in time for Masters weekend." Pitch angle for tech media: "This app uses on-device OCR to read handwritten golf scorecards and instantly calculate results across 25+ betting games." The tech angle (OCR on handwritten scorecards is genuinely interesting) gives ScoreSnap a story for tech publications that most golf apps can't tell.

3. **Golf Media Blitz.** Build relationships with editors and writers at: Golf Digest, MyGolfSpy, GolfWRX (forums + editorial), Golf.com, The Fried Egg, No Laying Up, and Golfweek. Provide early access + exclusive features/interviews. MyGolfSpy and GolfWRX are particularly valuable because their audiences are gear/tech-obsessed golfers who actively seek out new tools.

4. **Podcast Circuit.** Golf podcasts have passionate, loyal audiences. Target: No Laying Up (the "NPR of golf podcasts"), Chasing Scratch (two amateur golfers trying to reach scratch — exactly ScoreSnap's demographic), The Fried Egg Podcast, Erik Anders Lang's podcast. Pitch: the founder's story (why they built it, the OCR challenge, the 25+ game modes). For non-golf tech podcasts: pitch the technical story (building OCR for handwritten scorecards using on-device ML).

5. **Product Hunt & Hacker News Launch.** Product Hunt launch on Masters Monday (April 7). Prep: custom PH listing page, hunter lined up, community primed to upvote. Hacker News "Show HN" post focused on the technical OCR challenge. These channels drive developer/tech-forward early adopters who are often golfers themselves and who will provide high-quality feedback. A top-5 Product Hunt finish can drive 5K–10K installs in a single day.

**Weekly/Monthly Deliverables:** Media list with 50+ contacts (updated monthly), 3 pitch templates (launch story, feature story, trend piece), weekly coverage tracker, podcast outreach pipeline. Monthly: earned media value estimate, coverage analysis (which angles got pickup, which didn't), journalist relationship status.

**Case Studies to Model:**

- **Clubhouse's Invite-Only Pre-Launch:** Created artificial scarcity and exclusivity that generated millions in earned media coverage before the app was widely available. The waitlist itself became the story. Key learning: a waitlist with genuine benefits (not just a landing page) creates its own PR narrative.
- **Superhuman's Launch:** Combined a waitlist, referral system, and white-glove onboarding to create an exclusive launch that generated massive press coverage. Each user felt like a VIP. Key learning: treating early users as insiders creates organic advocacy that no press strategy can buy.

**Self-Improving Mechanism:** Track which pitch angles generate coverage (settle golf bets vs. OCR technology vs. founder story vs. game modes). Track referral traffic from each publication. Measure the install spike duration after each piece of coverage (some articles drive traffic for days, others for hours). Refine pitches based on what works and what journalists respond to.

**Experiment Backlog:**

- H1: The "settles your golf bets" angle generates 3x more journalist responses than the "OCR technology" angle
- H2: Product Hunt launch during Masters Week drives 2x more upvotes than a non-event launch date

### Experiment Tracking Framework

Every initiative is tracked as a formal experiment:

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **ID** | EXP-001, EXP-002, etc. |
| **Hypothesis** | "We believe [action] will cause [metric] to [change] because [reasoning]" |
| **Metric** | The specific number we're measuring |
| **Test Design** | What we're doing, control vs. variant |
| **Duration** | How long we'll run before evaluating |
| **Result** | Measured outcome with statistical confidence |
| **Learning** | What we now know that we didn't before |
| **Next Action** | Scale, iterate, or kill |

Example:

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **ID** | EXP-003 |
| **Hypothesis** | "We believe showing dollar amounts on results cards will increase share rate by 25% because financial stakes create more emotional engagement" |
| **Metric** | Share rate (% of completed rounds where results card is shared) |
| **Test Design** | A: Card with "$12 won" vs. B: Card with "Won Skins" (no dollar amount) |
| **Duration** | 2 weeks |
| **Result** | A: 34% share rate, B: 21% share rate — 62% improvement |
| **Learning** | Dollar amounts dramatically increase share motivation. Users want to brag about winnings specifically. |
| **Next Action** | Scale: all results cards now show dollar amounts by default. Next test: card design variations. |

### K-Factor Sensitivity Analysis

The viral coefficient (K-factor) is the single most important variable. K = (invites sent per user) × (conversion rate of invites).

| K-Factor | Organic Installs Needed | Viral Installs | Total | Feasibility |
|----------|------------------------|----------------|-------|-------------|
| 0.3 | 77,000 | 23,000 | 100,000 | Hard — requires very strong organic channels |
| 0.5 | 67,000 | 33,000 | 100,000 | Achievable — requires solid ASO + influencer + content |
| 0.7 | 59,000 | 41,000 | 100,000 | Good — viral loops meaningfully reduce organic pressure |
| 1.0 | 50,000 | 50,000 | 100,000 | Great — every organic install generates one viral install |
| 1.5 | 30,000 | 70,000 | 100,000 | Excellent — viral growth dominates, minimal organic needed |

**Target: K-factor of 0.7 by June.** This means the settlement loop, share cards, and referral program together generate 0.7 new installs for every user acquired through other channels.

### Risk Scenarios & Mitigations

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|------------|
| OCR doesn't ship on time or accuracy is insufficient | Medium | Critical | Ship manual entry as fallback; market the game mode engine independently of OCR |
| K-factor stays below 0.3 | Medium | High | Invest more heavily in ASO and influencer; A/B test share mechanics aggressively |
| Golf season weather delays (cold spring) | Low | Medium | Push content harder during indoor/simulator season; target southern markets first |
| App Store rejection or review delays | Low | High | Submit 3 weeks before target launch; have contingency dates |
| Competitor launches similar product | Low | Medium | Move fast; first-mover advantage in "scan and settle" is defensible with network effects |
| User confusion about game modes | Medium | Medium | Progressive disclosure in onboarding; "Game Mode of the Week" content series educates |

### The Math That Matters Most

If ScoreSnap averages 4 players per round, and each round has a 60% chance of producing at least 1 new install (through settlement, share card, or word of mouth), then:

- 1,000 rounds scanned = 600 new installs from viral alone
- 10,000 rounds scanned = 6,000 new installs from viral alone
- At 1,000 DAU each scanning once per week = 4,000 rounds/month = 2,400 viral installs/month

This is why the K-factor is the most important metric. Every improvement to the viral loop compounds across every round, every foursome, every course, every week of golf season.

**The path to 100K is not about finding 100,000 golfers. It's about finding the first 10,000 and letting the product do the rest.**
